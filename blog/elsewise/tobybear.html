Editing my Cat into a Painting

May 2, 2025

Last December, I went to the Crystal Bridges Art Museum. They had all sorts of interesting things there (like a pond intentionally filled with hundreds of large steel balls), but one exhibit in particular caught my attention.

It's a painting of a bear attacking a guy. Nothing special. What caught my attention wasn't what it was, but what it wasn't. I first saw the painting from across the gallery and I thought that the bear was a housecat. I realized my mistake when I moved closer, but the error stayed with me.

First Attempt

About a month after seeing the painting, I had the idea to edit my cat, Toby, into the painting in place of the bear.

At the time, I was still very new to photo editing, so my workflow was hacky.

To remove the bear, I uploaded the photo to Google Photos, used Magic Eraser to remove the bear, then downloaded it back to my computer.

Then I opened a photo of Toby in GIMP, manually traced a selection around Toby and deleted everything outside of that selection. I then foolishly exported it in the non-alpha-supporting JPEG format and had to do it again (I used the JPEG for the juxtapose below because its a smaller file).

Now that I had a transparent-background graphic of Toby, I pasted it onto the bear-less painting. I used GIMP's perspective transform tool to try and line up the corner, but it still looks wierd.

This is a completely acceptable result. It doesn't look convincing (Toby is hovering above the ground, the lighting is wrong, no eye contact, etc.), but that was never the point. It would be ridiculous to spend any more time on this.

Second Attempt

So today I spent some more time on it.
